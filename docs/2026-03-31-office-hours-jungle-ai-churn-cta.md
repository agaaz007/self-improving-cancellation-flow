## Problem Statement

Jungle AI and similar subscription edtech products lose users at the cancellation screen because the intervention is usually generic. The company has rich behavioral data, but the exit flow still behaves like a blunt instrument. The problem is to use real behavioral signals to decide what to show a canceling user: the right message angle, the right offer, and the right CTA.

## Demand Evidence

- This is already a live business problem, not a hypothetical one.
- You described an existing company workflow that tries to “intervene at the exit point where people are canceling their subscriptions.”
- The economic value is immediate: lower churn for a subscription business.
- The data exhaust already exists through Amplitude, Mixpanel, or PostHog, which means the wedge can start without inventing a new tracking layer.

## Status Quo

Today the likely workflow is some mix of:

- generic cancellation copy,
- one-size-fits-all discounting,
- manual retention ideas from growth teams, and
- no reliable way to know whether urgency, proof, empathy, or feature reminders should lead.

That means teams risk both under-personalizing and over-personalizing, while also burning margin with unnecessary discounts.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

Primary buyer/user:

- Growth lead, founder, or lifecycle PM at a subscription edtech company.

Narrowest wedge:

- Monthly subscription edtech apps with a cancellation page and enough user behavior data to infer study intent, usage depth, and activation pattern.

## Constraints

- Must work with existing behavioral data rather than requiring pristine new instrumentation
- Must avoid creepy over-personalization
- Must choose between multiple intervention levers, not just copy
- Must be fast enough to support exit-flow decisions
- Must start with synthetic cohorts before live experimentation

## Premises

1. Users cancel for different reasons, so one intervention policy will underperform.
2. Behavioral depth is a meaningful proxy for what kind of save attempt will work.
3. Offer choice is as important as copy choice.
4. A synthetic research loop is a valid starting point before live randomized tests.
5. Trust-safety matters; a “better” message can still be the wrong product move if it feels invasive.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Copy Generator First

Use an LLM to generate many personalized messages from user features, then rank them manually.

Why it is tempting:

- Fast to demo
- Looks personalized quickly

Why it is weak:

- Over-indexes on words, under-indexes on policy
- No explicit way to learn when not to discount
- Hard to debug why a message wins

### Approach B: Decision Lab First

Build a fixed evaluation harness that scores combinations of message angle, incentive, and CTA across a synthetic persona cohort derived from seed profiles.

Why it is stronger:

- Makes the decision surface explicit
- Easier to inspect, tune, and backtest
- Adapts well to the `autoresearch` pattern

## Recommended Approach

Start with Approach B.

Treat this as a retention decision lab, not a copy factory. Build a synthetic persona generator, derive interpretable features from analytics-style profiles, and score strategy combinations over a cohort. Use that to learn what kinds of interventions should exist for which kinds of canceling users. Then bring the top candidates into live experiments.

## Open Questions

- What exact historical outcome label can be recovered: retained 7 days, retained 30 days, accepted pause, accepted discount, reactivated later?
- Which product signals correlate most with genuine student urgency versus idle browsing?
- Should parents and student end-users be treated as separate messaging audiences?
- What are the legal or brand boundaries around using education outcome claims in cancellation copy?

## Success Criteria

- Produce segment-specific top strategies instead of one global generic strategy
- Show interpretable reasons for each recommendation
- Demonstrate that the top simulated strategies are materially different across seed personas
- Generate a workflow that can later be validated by offline backtests and live experiments

## Distribution Plan

## Dependencies

- Cleaned seed profile schema
- A small set of allowed offers and CTA actions
- Historical or expert feedback later for calibration
