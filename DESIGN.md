## Product Surface

This design system is for an internal research console used by growth, lifecycle, and retention teams. It is not consumer-facing. The UI should feel like a sharp analytical instrument, not a generic dashboard.

## Design Direction

Working title: **Signal Room**

- Mood: editorial analytics, calm urgency, high trust
- Visual metaphor: annotated lab notebook meets mission control
- Goal: help a human operator understand why a message strategy should win, not just that it scored well

## Typography

- Headline font: `Fraunces` or `Iowan Old Style`
- Interface font: `IBM Plex Sans`
- Tabular numerics: `IBM Plex Mono`

Use serif only for section headers and large score callouts. Use sans for interaction surfaces. Use mono only for metrics, cohort IDs, and experiment tags.

## Color System

- Ink: `#14213d`
- Slate: `#3d405b`
- Sand: `#f4f1de`
- Signal Green: `#3a7d44`
- Heat Amber: `#e09f3e`
- Risk Red: `#9d0208`
- Mist: `#e9ecef`

The product should avoid flat white backgrounds. Prefer layered sand, mist, and soft blueprint gradients.

## Layout

- Left rail: cohort filters, persona selector, experiment history
- Main canvas: top strategy cards, message previews, lift deltas
- Right rail: rationale, risk flags, trust-safety notes

Default desktop layout should show all three rails. On tablet, collapse the right rail into an expandable drawer. On mobile, prioritize persona summary -> top strategy -> rationale.

## Components

- Persona cards: photo-free, behavior-first, one emotional headline plus three factual signals
- Strategy cards: message angle, offer, CTA, predicted lift, trust score
- Experiment timeline: stacked chips with keep/discard markers
- Risk annotations: inline callouts for creepiness risk, over-discounting, and weak evidence

## Motion

- Staggered reveal for top strategies on load
- Subtle cross-fade when switching personas
- No decorative micro-animations on metrics

## Accessibility

- AA contrast minimum across all states
- Keyboard reachable comparison board
- No meaning communicated only by color
- Every score needs a text explanation

## UX Principles

- Show the reason before the number whenever possible
- Never hide uncertainty; surface it as a first-class design element
- Default to comparison, not isolated views
- Make “pause instead of cancel” feel like a product move, not a dark pattern

## Anti-Slop Rules

- No default admin template cards
- No interchangeable blue-purple SaaS look
- No anonymous “AI recommendation” badges without explanation
- No giant empty hero banner on the internal app
